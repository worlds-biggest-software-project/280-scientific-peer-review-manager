## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics) Guidelines** — ethical standards for peer review, conflict of interest disclosure, and editorial decision-making
- **ORCID integration** — researcher persistent identifier for author and reviewer disambiguation; standard across major submission systems
- **CrossRef / DOI assignment** — persistent identifier assignment at acceptance, increasingly integrated into editorial workflow systems
- **Crossref Similarity Check (iThenticate)** — industry-standard plagiarism detection integrated into most submission systems
- **JATS (Journal Article Tag Suite)** — XML standard for structuring journal article content, relevant to manuscript ingestion and export
- **ANSI/NISO Z39.96 JATS** — formal standard for journal article metadata and content tagging
- **Registered Reports** — peer review model where protocols are reviewed and accepted before data collection; some platforms now support this workflow
- **Open Peer Review standards** — growing set of transparency practices (open identities, open reports, open participation) being standardised by publishers

## Market Research

**Market Size:** The global academic publishing market exceeds USD 25 billion annually. The peer review management software segment (submission systems, editorial tools) is estimated at USD 500 million–USD 1 billion, growing at approximately 8–12% CAGR. AI integration is accelerating platform refresh cycles and new entrant investment.

**Funding:** Editorial Manager (Aries Systems) was acquired by Wolters Kluwer for a reported USD 110 million. ScholarOne is owned by Clarivate (market cap ~$3 billion). Scholastica raised approximately $7 million in venture funding. Kryoni and newer AI-native entrants are early-stage, venture-backed.

**Pricing Landscape:** Legacy enterprise platforms (Editorial Manager, ScholarOne) are custom-priced for large publishers on multi-year contracts, typically $10,000–$200,000+/yr depending on journal count and volume. Society and independent journals use Scholastica ($10/submission or custom), OJS (free), or Manuscript Manager (custom). AI reviewer-matching add-ons (Prophy) are licensed separately. Integrity tools (iThenticate) are typically subscription-based per submission.

**Key Buyer Personas:** Journal editors-in-chief managing manuscript flow and editorial decisions; managing editors and editorial assistants handling reviewer coordination and workflow; journal publishers managing portfolio-level submission systems; scholarly society executive directors overseeing flagship journal operations; research integrity officers monitoring for plagiarism and data manipulation.

**Notable Trends:** AI reviewer matching using semantic publication analysis (not just keyword matching) is the leading innovation in 2026, addressing the growing reviewer fatigue and declining invitation response rates. Automated image integrity checking is becoming standard before peer review assignment, not just at acceptance. Open peer review (transparent reviewer identities and reports) is expanding beyond pilot programmes. Reviewer load balancing algorithms are preventing over-invitation of the same reviewers. AI manuscript pre-screening for scope fit is being used to reduce editor workload before formal peer review.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- Semantic reviewer matching that analyses the full-text of submitted manuscripts against a database of researchers' publication records, identifying qualified reviewers beyond simple keyword or citation overlap, while actively managing workload distribution and conflict-of-interest detection
- Automated scope and quality pre-screening that evaluates submitted manuscripts for journal fit, methodological completeness, and statistical reporting standards before editor assignment, reducing desk rejection burden
- AI-assisted review quality monitoring that analyses submitted peer review reports for depth, specificity, and constructive content, flagging low-quality or boilerplate reviews before authors receive them
- Reviewer invitation optimisation that predicts reviewer acceptance likelihood based on historical response rates, time of year, current assignment load, and topical proximity, improving invitation efficiency
- Editorial decision support that synthesises multiple reviewer reports into a structured summary of consensus and disagreement across key dimensions (novelty, methodology, presentation, significance), helping editors make faster and more consistent decisions
